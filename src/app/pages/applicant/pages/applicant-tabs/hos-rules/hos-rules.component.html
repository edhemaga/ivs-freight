HOS Rules and Regulations

Hours of Service (HOS) are regulations governed by the FMCSA which is the abbreviation for the "Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration". The FM CSA is a division of the United States Department of Transportation (USDOT) which is responsible for code enforcement. "Hours of Service" refers to the hours that a commercial driver operating a commercial motor vehicle on the road can operate during a 70 hours within 8 days period without resting for 34 hours before continuing. Drivers are responsible for maintaining a record of their hours in a logbook while operating detailing hours driving and resting.

The basic definitions and interpretations of the new HOS Rules and Regulations are as follows: Note: Drivers have two sets of time in one period. Drive time and on duty time during the period. Drive time+ on duty time = total time towards the 14 hour daily on duty limit. A period maxed out is 70 hours or 8 consecutive days, whichever comes first.

Truck drivers may not drive more than 11 hours resting for 30 minutes before continuing after 8 hours driving

Truck drivers may not drive after 14 hours of on duty time.

After 70 hours of on duty time within 8 days the driver must take 34 hours off that include 2 - separate 8 hour sleeping shifts that have to include sleep during the hours of 1 am to 5 am. Yes HOS determines what time of the day you will sleep.

What this means is after 8 hours of off duty time drivers need to take a 30 minute break that counts against your on duty time (14 hours) then may drive no more than 3 more hours within 14 hours' time. The 14 hours' time can't be extended by 8 hours in the sleeper and 2 hours off duty time - 10 hours total. Time in the sleeper (8 hours continuous) + 2 hours off duty is the only way to recharge for another 14 hours of time on duty time. Each period while the driver is on duty can't exceed whichever is soonest 70 hours in 8 days. To start another period the driver must take 34 hours of off duty time.

THE SLEEPER BERTH PROVISION.

You can use the sleeper berth provision to get you on the road again if you have time left in your 70 hour within 8 day period but you have to sleep for a minimum of 8 consecutive hours and then be off duty for 2 hours.

70 HOURS IN 8 DAYS RULE.

After a combined total of drive time and on duty time it can't exceed 70 hours within 8 days' time. As soon as you hit 70 hours or 8 days the driver can't drive for 34 hours of time off.

34 HOURS RECHARGE RULE.

After a driver has been on duty for his combined total of either 70 hours within/or 8 days the period is over. You can start another period with 34 hours off but once again you have to get 2 different sleep shifts that MUST CONTAIN sleep within the hours of 1 am to 5 am in each sleep. Yes you ready that correctly HOS controls when you can sleep within 4 hours. Off means off. No paperwork, tarping, shining chrome. Go spend time with your family, friends or just go clear your head. You are important to the nation and we need you healthy so we can all function with whatever it is you are delivering. We love, care about and thank the commercial drivers of America!
